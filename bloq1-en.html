Fabric Dye Guide

Can You Use Viktoria Fabric Dye in a Metal Bowl or Pot?

Short answer: Yes — but only with the right type of metal. Stainless steel (inox) and enamel-coated pots are safe. Aluminum and copper should always be avoided.

Viktoria fabric dye is one of the most trusted clothing dyes available in Azerbaijan. Whether you want to refresh a faded black shirt or completely change the color of a garment, Viktoria dye delivers vibrant, long-lasting results at home — without expensive equipment.

One of the most common questions we receive is: "Can I dye clothes in my regular kitchen pot?" The answer depends on the type of metal.

✅ Safe Metal Containers for Fabric Dyeing

  • Stainless steel (inox) pots — The safest and most recommended option. Does not react with dye.
  • Enamel-coated pots — Also safe, as the coating acts as a barrier between the metal and the dye.

❌ Metal Containers to Avoid

  • Aluminum pots — React chemically with fabric dye and may damage the fabric color or the pot itself.
  • Copper pots — Can cause unwanted color shifts and chemical reactions with the dye solution.
⚠️ Important: Never use the same pot for cooking after it has been used for fabric dyeing. Dye residue absorbs into metal surfaces permanently. Always keep a dedicated pot for dyeing only.

🧵 Step-by-Step: How to Dye Clothes at Home with Viktoria Dye

  1. Fill a stainless steel or enamel pot with enough warm water to fully submerge the fabric.
  2. Dissolve the Viktoria fabric dye completely in the water before adding clothes.
  3. Pre-wet the garment with water, then submerge it fully in the dye bath.
  4. Stir constantly with a wooden or plastic spoon for even color coverage.
  5. Leave the fabric in the dye for 20 to 40 minutes, depending on desired intensity.
  6. Remove and rinse in cold water until water runs clear.
  7. Wash separately in cold water for the first 2–3 washes.
💡 Pro tip: For a deeper, richer color, add a tablespoon of salt to the dye bath before adding the fabric. This helps the dye bond more effectively to natural fibers like cotton.
